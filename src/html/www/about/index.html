About

About me

Hey! My name is Jake, and I'm a recent Computer Science graduate from Oklahoma State. I've always been the type to take interest in things with flashing lights and bright colors, as long as I get to put it together myself. I began programming when I was 18—creating games in frameworks such as libGDX or XNA—and gained an interest in web design somewhere along the way. Nowadays, you can find me working on front-end UI, game engine architecture, or playing around with a Raspberry Pi. My language of choice right now is probably TypeScript, although I'm not afraid to take the plunge into a C/C++ codebase.

Other interests of mine include drawing, hiking, watching football, and gazing at planes as they fly overhead. I like keeping myself active by participating in mud-runs or playing rugby, but I really just enjoy doing anything where I get an excuse to run. Climbing mountains is what I dream of doing everyday, and a good bowl of pasta will always put me in a great mood.

About this site

This website is all about learning! Sure, using a framework such as Bootstrap or Foundation would have made the development of this site much easier, but where's the fun in that? I've had a much better time trying to figure out things like absolute positioning or vertical margin collapsing by myself1. Naturally, this site has become my personal tool for learning the nitty-gritty details of web development. Experimental technologies are always welcome here, and will be used with reckless abandon, so don't be too surprised when WebAssembly becomes the backbone of this page! I try not to break the site too much, but it happens every so often.

In the spirit of learning, I've created a few "guidelines" for this site to abide by, which you can read about on the page linked below. Additionally, all source code relating to this site and its development can be viewed on GitHub. Feel free to stop on by!
